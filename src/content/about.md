I am Messi H.J. Lee, a researcher in *machine psychology*, a field dedicated to understanding the behavior and reasoning abilities of AI. I earned my PhD in Computational and Data Sciences from Washington University in St. Louis in May 2025, co-advised by [Calvin K. Lai](https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&user=Nkkrs_YAAAAJ) and [Jacob M. Montgomery](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=GaWC-J4AAAAJ). I use psychological frameworks to study how AI models develop and display human-like social biases.

My recent work investigates [implicit bias-like patterns in reasoning models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.11572). I found that these systems require significantly more computational power to process information that challenges societal stereotypes, such as men with family and/or women with careers. This suggests that even advanced AI experiences cognitive friction similar to human implicit bias when navigating counter-stereotypical information. 

Currently, I am completing my military service in South Korea as technical research personnel (전문연구요원) developing AI for pathology. My focus is identifying bias in diagnostic models to ensure equitable healthcare results.
